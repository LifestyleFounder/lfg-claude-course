## Content & Social (1-10)

### 1. Weekly Content Ideas
```
Give me 7 content ideas for this week — one for each day. Each idea should include: the hook (first line), the angle (what makes it interesting), and which platform it's best for. Make them relevant to what my ideal clients are struggling with right now.
```

### 2. Scroll-Stopping Hooks
```
Write me 10 scroll-stopping hooks for [TOPIC]. Mix up the formats — some should be controversial takes, some curiosity-driven, some "I can't believe this works" style. Write them in my voice. No corporate fluff.
```

### 3. Instagram Carousel
```
Turn this coaching concept into a 7-slide Instagram carousel: [YOUR CONCEPT]. Slide 1 = hook that stops the scroll, Slides 2-6 = the transformation or framework, Slide 7 = CTA. Write the text for every slide. Keep it punchy and actionable.
```

### 4. Content Repurpose
```
Take this piece of content and repurpose it for 4 platforms — Instagram caption, LinkedIn post, email newsletter, and 60-second video script. Same core message, adapted for each platform's vibe: [PASTE CONTENT]
```

### 5. Client Transformation Post
```
Turn this client result into a compelling story post: [DESCRIBE THE RESULT]. Structure: the "before" (where they were stuck), the shift (what changed), the "after" (the result), and a soft CTA. Make it feel real, not salesy.
```

### 6. Workshop Promo Sequence
```
Write me 5 social media posts to promote my upcoming workshop on [TOPIC]. Post 1 = curiosity hook, Post 2 = pain point, Post 3 = social proof, Post 4 = behind-the-scenes, Post 5 = last call. Space them across a week.
```

### 7. Video Script (60 seconds)
```
Write me a 60-second video script about [TOPIC]. Structure: Hook (first 3 seconds — make them stop scrolling), 3 quick coaching insights, CTA. Write it exactly how I talk. No filler. No "in this video I'm going to..."
```

### 8. Engagement Post Ideas
```
Give me 10 social media post ideas designed to spark comments and conversation. Mix of: polls, "hot takes" about coaching, "which would you choose" scenarios, questions my ideal clients would love to answer, and controversial opinions I actually hold.
```

### 9. Content Calendar
```
Build me a 30-day content calendar. Mix content types: coaching tips, client wins, behind-the-scenes, framework breakdowns, myth-busting, and engagement posts. Include the hook for each one. Format as a table with Day, Type, Hook, and Platform.
```

### 10. Story Sequence
```
Write a 5-story Instagram story sequence that sells my coaching without being salesy. Start with a relatable problem my clients face, show the transformation, end with a soft CTA. Include poll and question sticker ideas to boost engagement.
```

## Client Communication (11-18)

### 11. New Client Welcome
```
Write a personalized onboarding message for a new coaching client. Include: a warm welcome, what to expect in the first week, how to get the most out of working with me, and one quick win they can do today. Make it feel like a friend welcoming them, not a corporate email.
```

### 12. Discovery Call Questions
```
Give me 5 smart intake questions for [NICHE] prospects who are considering coaching. These should uncover: their real problem (not the surface one), what they've already tried, what success looks like, their timeline, and their biggest fear about investing. Make them conversational.
```

### 13. Client Check-In
```
Write a weekly check-in message I can send to coaching clients. It should feel caring and personal, not scripted. Ask about their progress, celebrate any wins (even small ones), and gently redirect if they're stuck. Keep it under 100 words.
```

### 14. Milestone Celebration
```
Write a congratulations message for a client who just [DESCRIBE THE WIN]. Make it specific, genuine, and hype them up. Reference their journey — where they started vs where they are now. End with encouragement for what's next.
```

### 15. Workshop Follow-Up
```
Write a follow-up message for someone who attended my workshop on [TOPIC] but didn't sign up for coaching. Acknowledge what they learned, reference a specific moment from the workshop, and open the door for a conversation — no pressure. Give me 3 versions: short, medium, and story-based.
```

### 16. Re-Engagement Message
```
Write a message for a former client (or dormant lead) I haven't talked to in a while. It should feel natural — not "just checking in" generic. Reference something relevant to their goals, share a quick insight, and invite a conversation. No pitch. Just reconnection.
```

### 17. Testimonial Request
```
Write a message I can send to a happy client asking for a testimonial. Make it easy for them — include 3 specific questions they can answer instead of staring at a blank page: 1) Where were you before we started? 2) What shifted? 3) What's different now? Keep the ask casual and grateful.
```

### 18. Referral Request
```
Write a warm referral request I can send to my best clients. Acknowledge the results they've gotten, explain who else I can help (describe my ideal client in plain language), and make it easy — give them a message they can literally forward. No awkwardness.
```

## Strategy & Business (19-26)

### 19. Offer Audit
```
Here's my current coaching offer: [DESCRIBE IT — price, what's included, duration, format]. Audit it honestly. What's strong? What's weak? What would you add, remove, or reposition? How does the price-to-value ratio feel? Give me specific, actionable suggestions.
```

### 20. Competitor Analysis
```
Analyze these 3 coaches/programs in my space: [LIST THEM]. For each one: what they do well, what they're missing, how they position themselves, their pricing if visible. Then tell me how I'm different and where I can win.
```

### 21. Lead Magnet Ideas
```
Give me 10 lead magnet ideas for my coaching audience. For each one include: the title, format (PDF, quiz, video, template, etc.), what problem it solves, and a one-line hook I'd use to promote it. Rank them by likely conversion potential.
```

### 22. Pricing Strategy
```
I currently charge [CURRENT PRICING] for [COACHING OFFER]. Analyze my pricing. Am I leaving money on the table? Should I restructure into tiers? Give me 3 pricing models to consider, with pros and cons for each. Be honest about what the market will bear.
```

### 23. Client Avatar Deep Dive
```
Build me a detailed ideal client profile. Include: demographics, psychographics (what keeps them up at night), their top 5 pain points, what they've already tried that didn't work, what would make them invest immediately, where they hang out online, and the exact language they use to describe their problems.
```

### 24. Workshop Outline
```
Create a complete workshop outline for [TOPIC]. Include: title options (3), the promise/hook, 3-4 teaching sections with key points, an exercise or interaction moment, and the transition to my coaching offer. Make it valuable enough to stand alone but naturally lead to wanting more.
```

### 25. 90-Day Growth Plan
```
Build me a 90-day growth plan for [GOAL]. Break it into 3 phases (30 days each). Each phase needs: 3-5 specific actions with deadlines, the metric to track, and what success looks like. Make it realistic for a coach who works 4 days a week.
```

### 26. Weekly CEO Brief
```
Act as my business strategist. Based on everything you know about my coaching business, give me a weekly brief: Top 3 priorities this week, 1 thing I should stop doing, 1 opportunity I'm probably missing, and a reality check on whether I'm focused on the right things. Keep it real.
```

## Systems & Operations (27-30)

### 27. Client Onboarding SOP
```
Write a step-by-step client onboarding process for my coaching program. From the moment they say "yes" to their first week of coaching. Include: the welcome message, what to send them, account setup, first assignment, and the check-in schedule. Make it so clear that anyone on my team could follow it.
```

### 28. Discovery Call Prep
```
I have a discovery call with [TYPE OF PROSPECT] about [THEIR SITUATION]. Prep me: 3 things to research about them beforehand, 5 questions to ask (that uncover the real problem), the top 3 objections they'll probably have and how to handle each, and a follow-up message template for after the call.
```

### 29. FAQ Page
```
Write 10 frequently asked questions for my coaching business with answers. Cover: who this is for, what's included, how it works, time commitment, results timeline, pricing philosophy, what makes me different, refund policy, getting started, and "is this right for me?" Write the answers in my voice — honest, direct, no BS.
```

### 30. Automate My Week
```
Based on my tools and what I do as a coach, identify 10 tasks I'm probably doing manually that could be automated or templated. For each one: what the task is, how long it probably takes me now, what the automated version looks like, and which tools to use. Prioritize by time saved per week.
```
