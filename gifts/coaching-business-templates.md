# Coaching Business Templates

10 mega-prompts that each build a complete system for your coaching business. One paste = one complete output. All personalized because of your CLAUDE.md.

**How to use:** Copy any prompt below. Paste it into Claude Code. Get a complete, ready-to-use system back. Edit as needed and deploy.

---

## 1. 30-Day Content Calendar

```
Build me a complete 30-day content calendar for my coaching business.

For each day include:
- Platform (Instagram, LinkedIn, email, video — mix it up)
- Content type (tip, story, framework, client win, behind-the-scenes, engagement, myth-bust)
- The hook (first line — make it a scroll-stopper)
- The angle (2 sentences on what the post covers)
- CTA (what I want them to do after)

Format as a clean table. Make sure there's variety — no two consecutive days should have the same type. Reference my ideal clients and their specific pain points throughout. End with 5 "emergency" post ideas I can use anytime I'm stuck.
```

## 2. Client Onboarding Workflow

```
Build me a complete client onboarding workflow for my coaching program.

Include every step from "they said yes" to "first week complete":
1. Immediate response (within 1 hour of payment)
2. Welcome message (personalized, warm, sets expectations)
3. Account setup instructions (what to access, how to log in)
4. First assignment or quick win (something they can do TODAY)
5. Day 2 check-in message
6. Day 3-4 resource drop (send them something useful)
7. Day 7 first-week recap + what's next

For each step: the exact message/template, when to send it, and what tool to use. Make it so detailed that someone on my team could run it without asking me a single question.
```

## 3. Sales Page Copy

```
Write complete sales page copy for my coaching offer.

Include these sections in order:
1. Hero headline + subheadline (3 options each)
2. Opening story (the problem my clients face — make it visceral)
3. "The real problem" section (dig deeper than the surface)
4. The solution (my coaching approach — without being salesy)
5. What's included (bullet points with benefit-driven descriptions)
6. Who this is for / who this is NOT for
7. The transformation (before vs after — specific and believable)
8. 3 testimonial frameworks (realistic scenarios I can fill in with real client stories)
9. FAQ section (8 questions with honest, personality-driven answers)
10. Final CTA section (urgency without fake scarcity)

Write everything in my voice. No corporate speak. No hype. Just honest, direct copy that sounds like me talking to a friend who needs help.
```

## 4. 5-Email Welcome Sequence

```
Write a complete 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers to my list.

Email 1 (Day 0) — Welcome + Quick Win
- Subject line options (3)
- Deliver on whatever they signed up for
- Give them one actionable insight they can use TODAY
- Set expectations for what's coming

Email 2 (Day 2) — My Story
- Why I do what I do
- The moment that changed everything for me
- Connect my story to THEIR struggle

Email 3 (Day 4) — The Biggest Mistake
- The #1 mistake I see in my coaching niche
- Why most people get this wrong
- The simple shift that fixes it

Email 4 (Day 7) — Client Win Story
- A detailed client transformation (framework — fill in with real details)
- What they were struggling with → what shifted → where they are now
- Soft CTA to learn more

Email 5 (Day 10) — The Invitation
- Acknowledge they've been reading
- Paint the picture of what's possible
- Extend the invitation to work together — no pressure, just clarity
- Clear next step

For each email: subject line (3 options), preview text, full body copy, and PS line. Write in my voice — conversational, direct, zero fluff.
```

## 5. Social Media Bio Pack

```
Write optimized bios for all my social media platforms.

For each platform, write 3 options:

Instagram (150 char max):
- Version A: Credibility-led (results + what I do)
- Version B: Personality-led (voice + vibe)
- Version C: CTA-led (what they get by following)

LinkedIn (220 char headline + 2000 char about):
- Professional but still sounds like me
- Lead with outcomes, not titles
- Include a clear CTA

TikTok (80 char max):
- Punchy and curiosity-driven
- Makes people want to see the next video

YouTube (1000 char about):
- What the channel is about
- Who it's for
- What they'll get
- Upload schedule (if applicable)
- CTA to subscribe

Twitter/X (160 char max):
- Sharp and memorable
- Personality forward

Make sure every bio is consistent in positioning but adapted for each platform's culture. Use my actual results and credentials from CLAUDE.md.
```

## 6. Weekly Newsletter Template

```
Build me a reusable weekly newsletter template for my coaching audience.

Structure:
1. Subject line formula (with 5 examples)
2. Opening hook (3 formulas I can rotate: story, question, bold statement)
3. Main insight (the one thing I'm teaching this week)
4. Real example or analogy (make it relatable to my audience)
5. Action step (one thing they can do THIS WEEK)
6. Resource or tool recommendation (optional section)
7. PS line (personal note, upcoming event, or soft pitch)

Then write 4 COMPLETE example newsletters using this template, each on a different coaching topic relevant to my niche. I should be able to use these immediately or adapt them.

Keep it under 500 words per newsletter. Nobody reads long emails.
```

## 7. Lead Magnet + Landing Page Copy

```
Design a lead magnet and write the complete landing page copy for it.

Step 1 — Lead Magnet Design:
- Analyze my ideal client's top 3 pain points
- Suggest the best format (PDF guide, checklist, quiz, template, video training)
- Create the title (3 options — each should feel like a no-brainer to download)
- Write the complete outline (what's inside, section by section)

Step 2 — Landing Page Copy:
- Headline (3 options)
- Subheadline
- 3 bullet points of what they'll learn/get
- "Who this is for" section (3-4 bullet points)
- Social proof line (even if it's "Join 500+ coaches who downloaded this")
- CTA button text (3 options)
- Thank you page copy (what to expect after downloading)

Make the lead magnet genuinely valuable — something they'd pay for. The landing page should convert without feeling salesy.
```

## 8. Client Proposal Template

```
Write a professional coaching proposal template I can customize for each prospect.

Include:
1. Personalized opening (reference their specific situation — leave [BRACKETS] for customization)
2. "What I heard" section (reflecting back their problem — shows I listened)
3. "The opportunity" (reframe their problem as an opportunity)
4. My recommended approach (how my coaching addresses their specific needs)
5. What's included (detailed breakdown with benefits, not just features)
6. Timeline and milestones (what the first 30/60/90 days look like)
7. Investment section (how to present pricing with confidence)
8. Next steps (exactly what happens when they say yes)
9. FAQ mini-section (3 questions they're probably thinking)
10. Personal note (why I'm excited to work with them)

Format it so it looks clean and professional. Include instructions for how to customize each section for different prospects. This should take me less than 10 minutes to personalize per prospect.
```

## 9. FAQ Page (Website Ready)

```
Write a complete FAQ page for my coaching business website.

Write 10 questions and answers covering:
1. "What exactly do you do?" (clear, jargon-free explanation)
2. "Who is this for?" (specific — they should self-select in or out)
3. "Who is this NOT for?" (equally specific — saves everyone time)
4. "What results can I expect?" (honest, specific, with a timeline)
5. "How is this different from other coaching programs?" (my unique positioning)
6. "What's the time commitment?" (realistic and encouraging)
7. "How much does it cost?" (frame the investment conversation)
8. "What if it doesn't work for me?" (address the fear directly)
9. "How do I get started?" (clear next step)
10. "Can I talk to a past client?" (social proof + accessibility)

Write the answers in my voice — direct, honest, occasionally funny. Each answer should be 3-5 sentences max. No corporate speak. These should feel like me answering questions over coffee, not a legal document.
```

## 10. Competitor Analysis Report

```
Run a detailed competitor analysis for my coaching niche.

For each of the top 5 competitors you can identify (use WebSearch):
1. Who they are and what they offer
2. Their positioning (how do they describe what they do?)
3. Their pricing (if visible)
4. Their strengths (what they do well)
5. Their weaknesses (where they fall short)
6. Their content strategy (what platforms, what style)
7. What their clients say (testimonials, reviews, complaints)

Then give me:
- My unfair advantages (based on my CLAUDE.md)
- 3 positioning opportunities they're all missing
- Messaging angles I should steal (ethically)
- The one thing I should NEVER copy from them
- A one-paragraph positioning statement that makes me the obvious choice

Be specific. Use real data where possible. I want to understand the landscape, not just get generic advice.
```
